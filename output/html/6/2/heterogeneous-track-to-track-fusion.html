Heterogeneous Track-to-Track Fusion

Ting Yuan, Yaakov Bar-Shalom and Xin Tian

Read Article

Abstract

Track-to-track fusion using estimates from multiple sensors can achieve better estimation performance than single sensor track- ing. If the local sensors use different system models in different state spaces, the problem of heterogeneous track-to-track fusion arises. Compared with homogeneous track-to-track fusion that as- sumes the same system model for different sensors, the heteroge- neous case poses two major challenges. The first one is that we have to fuse estimates from different state spaces (related by a cer- tain nonlinear transformation). The second is the estimation errors’ dependence problem, which is generally recognized as the “com- mon process noise effect.” Different heterogeneous track-to-track fusion approaches, namely, the linear minimum mean square er- ror approach and the maximum likelihood approach, are presented and compared with the corresponding centralized measurement tracker/fuser (also known as measurement-to-track fuser).
